About me

Who am I, and why should you care?

I’m Neil, a third year Computer Science Major at the Illinois Institute of Technology, Chicago. I’m an aspiring Software Engineer and Game Developer, and my goal is to bring creativity and passion to my projects. In the process I hone my skills while I bring fresh and unique perspectives to the task at hand. Outside of programming, I entertain a wide spectrum of interests such as amateur boxing, bodybuilding, philosophy, journalism, and fictional writing. I’m privileged to have traveled extensively and I believe my experiences with different cultures have contributed greatly towards my personal development. As a first generation immigrant in the United States, I hope to make my family proud of the path I’ve chosen to pursue.

DMT Hackathon Grand Prize

My most memorable accomplishment at IIT was participating in the Dare Mighty Things Hackathon in 2019, hosted at the Mcdonalds HQ in Chicago. My teammates Mark Klinchin, Gabriel Bryk, Tommy Vadakumchery, and I walked away with both the categorical and grand prizes. It was no easy task as we were pitched against over twenty teams, many of whom were post-grad students. We were awarded certificates from JLL. The project files can be found on my github linked below.

Current Projects

Collaborating with the Aurora Australis team to design the MMORPG Zenerith, under the mentorship of Jonathan “Vorlias” Holmes. Holmes is an experienced software engineer with a multitude of successful games under his belt such as "Islands", which has received over 1.5 billion plays and over 20,000 concurrent players. The first iteration of Zenerith received 3.5 million plays. This project has provided me with invaluable experience in constructing a multiplayer game from the ground up.

Other Projects - Github

Aside from video game development, I have experience with web development, app development, databases, and other related topics. Some of my more memorable projects include contributions toward Chinatown's Chinese-American Museum website and my recent app prototype which assists nurses with managing their mental health, both of which were my way of giving back to the community. These and other projects can be viewed on my github.
