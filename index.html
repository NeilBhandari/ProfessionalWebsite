About me

Who am I, and why should you care?

I’m Neil, a third year Computer Science Major at the Illinois Institute of Technology, and an aspiring Software Engineer and Game Developer. My goal is to bring my innate creativity and passion to a multitude of projects. This accomplishes two major things: it furthers and potentially evolves the projects in question, and it imparts upon me tons of experience that I can apply later down the line. I find that I can provide a fresh and unique perspective to almost any software-related task sent my way. Outside of programming, I make sure to entertain a wide spectrum of interests such as amateur boxing, bodybuilding, philosophy, journalism, and fictional writing. I have traveled to around 10 or so countries on 4 different continents, and I believe my experiences while traveling have given me an upper hand in many surprising ways. As a first generation immigrant in the United States, I hope to make my family proud and supportive of the path I’ve chosen to pursue.

DMT Hackathon Grand Prize

One of my greatest accomplishments at IIT was winning both the categorical prize and grand prize at the Dare Mighty Things Hackathon in 2019, hosted at the Mcdonalds HQ in Chicago. We competed against over 100 competitors, including masters students. This victory was all thanks to the collaborative efforts with my teammates Mark Klinchin, Gabriel Bryk, and Tommy Vadakumchery. We were awarded and given certificates from JLL. The project files can be found on my github linked below.

Current Projects

My biggest current project is working with the Aurora Australis team to design the massively multiplayer online role-playing game Zenerith. The first iteration of Zenerith received 3.5 million plays. I am currently being mentored about basic video game design and structure by Jonathan “Vorlias” Holmes, who is an experienced software engineer with a multitude of successful games under his belt such as "Islands" which has received over 1.5 billion plays and had over 20,000 concurrent players. This project has provided me with invaluable experience in building a multiplayer game experience from the ground up.

Other Projects - Github

It goes without saying that a hefty amount of my projects are not video game related. I have plenty of experience with web development, app development, databases, and more. Some of these projects can be found on my github. I find it very fulfilling to participate in projects that give back to the community, such as in my contributions toward the Chinatown-American museum website, or in my recent app prototype project which assists nurses with their mental health.
